# 06 — Books and Publications: A Reading Library for Zentangle

Zentangle is one of the most book-friendly art forms in modern craft. Its short, repeatable steps; its tile-sized output; and its commitment to "anyone can do this" all map cleanly onto print formats — instructional books, pattern dictionaries, daily-practice journals, card decks, and gift books. The result is a publishing landscape with three distinct tiers: **(A)** official books by the founders, **(B)** major books authored by Certified Zentangle Teachers, and **(C)** Chinese-language books for the Taiwanese and broader Sinophone market.

This chapter surveys the most important titles in each tier and ends with a recommended reading order for beginners.

---

## A. Official Books by Rick Roberts and Maria Thomas

The founders publish under their own imprint, **Zentangle Books & Creations**, distributed through [zentangle.com](https://zentangle.com/) and select retailers.

### *The Book of Zentangle* (2012)

- **Authors:** Rick Roberts and Maria Thomas
- **Publisher:** Zentangle, Inc.
- **ISBN-13 (paperback):** 978-0-9859614-4-2
- **ISBN-13 (hardcover):** 978-0-9859614-0-4
- **Pages:** 146, full-color, ~6 × 9.5 inches.

This is the **canonical, definitive introduction** to the Zentangle Method, written entirely by the founders. It is part memoir (how Rick and Maria discovered the method), part philosophical statement (why "deliberate strokes" and "anything is possible, one stroke at a time" matter), and part practical manual (the Eight Steps, dozens of foundational tangles, full-page examples). If a student is going to own only one Zentangle book, this is the one.

### *Zentangle Primer, Vol. 1* (2016)

- **Authors:** Rick Roberts and Maria Thomas
- **Publisher:** Zentangle, Inc.
- **ISBN-13:** 978-0-9859614-5-9

A more workbook-style follow-up. Where *The Book of Zentangle* tells the story, *Primer* drills the technique — with extended step-outs, deeper instruction on shading, and more of the founders' personal teaching voice. Many CZTs use *Primer* as their textbook for an introductory four-week course.

### Project Pack books

Beginning around 2018, Rick and Maria launched the **Project Pack** model: a roughly bi-monthly themed kit (often a holiday, a season, or a special tile shape such as Zendala or Bijou) consisting of an instructional video series and a printed booklet. Project Packs are not always given ISBNs and are sold as supplies more than as books, but the printed booklets — there are now more than thirty — collectively form a sizeable library of seasonal, advanced, and experimental projects. They are most easily acquired via the Zentangle store.

### "Tangle of the Day" cards and small print items

Zentangle also produces card decks and small-format printed items: *Apprentice* kits for children, *Bijou* kits with miniature tiles, gift cards, and the *365 Tangles* daily desk calendar in some years. These are companions to the main books rather than standalone reads.

### *zenAgain* lecture compilations

Each annual ZenAgain CZT reunion produces extensive printed handouts and, in some years, a bound compilation of the founders' lectures and special-guest sessions. These are distributed to attendees only and circulate informally among CZTs; they are not for sale to the public.

## B. Major Books by Certified Zentangle Teachers

Outside of the founders, dozens of CZTs have published trade books — typically through **Quarry Books** (an imprint of Quarto), **Design Originals** (an imprint of Fox Chapel), or self-publishing. The titles below are the ones most consistently recommended inside the community.

### Beckah Krahula

**Beckah Krahula** is one of the original cohort of CZTs and a longtime mixed-media artist.

- ***One Zentangle a Day: A 6-Week Course in Creative Drawing for Relaxation, Inspiration, and Fun*** (Quarry Books, 2012)
  - **ISBN-13:** 978-1-59253-811-9
  - **Format:** ~144 pages, paperback, full color.
  - Structured as six chapters of seven days each — a forty-two-day program that walks beginners through the Zentangle Method, then nudges them into experiments with color, mixed media, and Zentangle-Inspired Art (ZIA). It is the single best-selling Zentangle book outside of the founders' own and the most common library acquisition.

### Sandy Steen Bartholomew

**Sandy Steen Bartholomew, CZT** is an illustrator and mixed-media artist whose work pairs Zentangle with collage and altered-book aesthetics.

- ***Totally Tangled: Zentangle and Beyond*** (Design Originals, 2010) — the breakthrough mass-market Zentangle book; introduced "Zentangle-Inspired Art" to the wider craft audience.
- ***Yoga for Your Brain: A Zentangle Workout*** (Design Originals, 2011) — the sequel, with 60+ tangle patterns plus tips and projects for experienced tanglers.
- ***Yoga for Your Brain — Totally Tangled Edition: Tangle Cards*** (Design Originals, 2013) — a portable deck of 40 tangle cards in a case, ISBN 978-1-57421-357-7. Plus *Original Edition* and *Kidz Edition* card decks.
- ***Joy of Zentangle*** (Design Originals, 2013, co-authored with Suzanne McNeill and Marie Browning) — a collaborative anthology that became the standard "first book" for many craft-store buyers.

### Suzanne McNeill (Design Originals founder)

**Suzanne McNeill, CZT** founded Design Originals and has authored or edited the largest single Zentangle book series on the market. Her *Zentangle* series (*Zentangle Basics*, *Zentangle 2*, *Zentangle 3* … through the early teens) functions as a numbered library, each volume introducing roughly 30 tangles plus project ideas.

- ***Zentangle Basics*** (Design Originals, 2010)
- ***Zentangle 2 / 3 / 4 / … / 13*** (Design Originals, 2010s) — incremental volumes, each narrowly themed (alphabet tangles, kids' tangles, monograms, etc.).

### Margaret Bremner

**Margaret Bremner, CZT** of Saskatchewan, Canada, is one of the most prolific tangle creators in the community (e.g., *Auraknot*, *Diva Dance*). She publishes primarily through her [Enthusiastic Artist blog](https://enthusiasticartist.blogspot.com/) but has contributed chapters and tutorials to many anthology volumes and is widely cited in CZT teaching.

### Anya Lothrop

**Anya Lothrop, CZT** is one of the principal voices for Zentangle in the German-speaking world. She has authored multiple German-language books published by **frechverlag (Topp)**, including step-by-step instruction books and themed project books that introduced Zentangle to a generation of German crafters.

### Helen Williams

**Helen Williams, CZT** is based in Tasmania, Australia and runs the influential blog [Pegs to Paper](https://pegs-to-paper.blogspot.com/). She is best known for her exquisite black-and-white linework and for several self-published instructional eBooks. She is the de facto godmother of the Australian Zentangle scene.

### Rebecca Kuan

**Rebecca Kuan, CZT** is a prolific Asian-Pacific CZT who has written across English and Chinese for years; her tangles (e.g., *Hibred*) are widely taught.

### Cris Letourneau

**Cris Letourneau, CZT** is one of the original cohort and a longtime Zentangle teacher and writer in the United States; her *Tangle Tour* and travel-themed teaching are well-known references inside the community.

### Linda Farmer — *TanglePatterns String Theory* series

**Linda Farmer, CZT** runs [TanglePatterns.com](https://tanglepatterns.com/), the largest and most-cited online tangle dictionary in any language. She self-publishes a series of practical workbook PDFs called *TanglePatterns String Theory*, organized by theme (e.g., flowers, fauna, geometry). These are sold direct from her site and are universally recommended supplemental references.

## C. Chinese-Language Books (Taiwan and Sinophone)

The Chinese-language Zentangle publishing scene is centered in Taiwan, with books by Taiwanese CZTs and authorized Chinese translations of Western titles. Major publishers include **遠流出版 (Yuan-Liou)**, **三采文化 (San Tsai)**, **楓書坊 (Maplebook)**, and **積木文化 (Cube Press)**.

### Books by Taiwanese CZTs

- **《蘿拉老師教你畫禪繞（入門篇）》** by **蘿拉 (Laura Liu, CZT)** — Taiwan's foundational beginner's manual, since reissued in a revised edition. Twenty-four "string" exercises, basic tangle library, and step-up to intermediate techniques. Available on [博客來 books.com.tw](https://www.books.com.tw/products/0010633615).
- **《戴安老師教你畫禪繞（進階篇）》** by **戴安 Diana Tai (CZT #11)** — the advanced companion: step-by-step shading, the kaleidoscope (萬花筒) Zendala technique, and color application. [Books.com.tw listing](https://www.books.com.tw/products/0010656986).
- **《戴安老師教你畫禪繞：進階篇（圓磚）**》** by **戴安 Diana Tai** — a follow-up volume devoted to the round Zendala tile format. [Books.com.tw](https://www.books.com.tw/products/0010814130).

### Authorized Chinese translations

- **《禪繞畫之書》** — Chinese edition of *The Book of Zentangle* by Rick Roberts and Maria Thomas, translated into Chinese (translators include Laura, 戴寧, Diana Tai), published by **遠流出版 (Yuan-Liou)**, ISBN 978-9573277323, 148 pages. The most important single Chinese-language Zentangle book.
- **《心靜禪繞畫：一筆一畫，靜心紓壓》** — *Joy of Zentangle*, Chinese edition. Authors: Suzanne McNeill, Sandy Steen Bartholomew, Marie Browning. Translator: 許玥琋. Publisher: **三采文化 (San Tsai Publishing)**, ISBN 978-986-342-119-1, 160 pages.
- **《禪繞畫新手變達人的第一本書：155 個禪繞圖樣與延伸應用，完全圖解》** — comprehensive reference of 155 patterns, available via [博客來](https://www.books.com.tw/products/0010640327).
- **《100 個禪繞畫的創意練習》** — practice-oriented translation, distributed by 誠品 and other Taiwan retailers.
- **《禪繞畫生活：創造屬於自己的禪繞圖樣》** — applied-life translation aimed at integrating Zentangle into journaling and home decor.
- **《禪繞之美：137 位全球頂尖禪繞藝術家作品集》** — picture-book anthology of 137 international Zentangle artists, distributed via 三民書局.
- **《禪繞花園：ZIA 彩繪舒壓著色本》** — coloring book / ZIA workbook variant.

> **Verification note (verify before publication):** Several Chinese-language titles are reissued frequently and may have multiple editions. The exact author attribution on translated titles (translator vs. original author) varies between Taiwanese and mainland Chinese editions. Confirm ISBN and edition on books.com.tw or the publisher's site before quoting.

### Mainland Chinese editions

Mainland China has its own simplified-Chinese editions, mostly published by **人民邮电出版社** and **化学工业出版社**, and mostly translations of the same Western source titles. The traditional-Chinese Taiwan editions are generally more polished for the Taiwan audience and usually preferred by CZT teachers.

## Recommended Reading Order for Beginners

Where to start depends on your reading language and how you learn best. Here are three suggested paths.

### Path 1 — The English Reader (most efficient)

1. **Beckah Krahula — *One Zentangle a Day*** (Quarry Books, 2012). Forty-two days of structured practice. The fastest, friendliest on-ramp.
2. **Rick Roberts and Maria Thomas — *The Book of Zentangle*** (2012). After six weeks of practice, read the founders directly to understand the philosophy.
3. **Rick Roberts and Maria Thomas — *Zentangle Primer, Vol. 1*** (2016). Drills technique and prepares you for advanced work.
4. **Sandy Steen Bartholomew — *Totally Tangled* + *Yoga for Your Brain***. Expand into Zentangle-Inspired Art and color.
5. **Suzanne McNeill — *Zentangle Basics* / 2 / 3** (browse). Pick volumes by theme as your taste develops.
6. **Linda Farmer — *TanglePatterns String Theory* PDFs** (ongoing reference). Use as a working dictionary alongside [tanglepatterns.com](https://tanglepatterns.com/).

### Path 2 — The Traditional-Chinese (Taiwan) Reader

1. **《蘿拉老師教你畫禪繞（入門篇）》** — by Taiwan's first CZT. Most natural starting point for Taiwanese readers.
2. **《禪繞畫之書》** — Chinese edition of *The Book of Zentangle*. Read after a month of practice.
3. **《戴安老師教你畫禪繞（進階篇）》** — Diana Tai's advanced companion to Laura's beginner book.
4. **《心靜禪繞畫：一筆一畫，靜心紓壓》** — broader anthology by McNeill / Bartholomew / Browning in Chinese translation.
5. **《禪繞畫新手變達人的第一本書》** — 155-pattern reference for daily practice.
6. **TanglePatterns.com** — bookmark in English; the dictionary entries are easily understood with minimal English.

### Path 3 — The Reader Who Wants to Become a CZT

1. *The Book of Zentangle* + *Zentangle Primer Vol. 1* — these two are essential pre-reading for the seminar.
2. *One Zentangle a Day* — for steady daily practice in the months before the seminar.
3. **Project Pack booklets** from zentangle.com — to get a feel for the founders' current voice.
4. **TanglePatterns.com** — read at least 50 tangle entries closely before applying.
5. After certification: **the entire ZenAgain handout archive** distributed inside the CZT community.

---

A final note: the Zentangle bibliography is unusual in that nearly every author on the major lists is herself or himself a CZT. Reading these books is therefore a way of meeting the community in print before meeting them in person. Many readers report that after a year of reading Krahula, Bartholomew, Bremner, and the founders, walking into a CZT seminar feels less like meeting strangers and more like finally putting faces to a long-running pen-pal correspondence. That is, in its own quiet way, the genius of Zentangle's publishing model.
