# Community and Resources: A Navigator for the Zentangle World

The Zentangle community is one of the most decentralized, generous, and well-documented art communities online. From Linda Farmer's exhaustive pattern dictionary to the daily prompts of Square One, from Rick and Maria's official video library to a Reddit community sharing tiles in real time, the resources available to a curious tangler are vast — and sometimes overwhelming.

This page is a navigator. It points you to the official sources first, then to the community pillars that have shaped the practice, then to the working channels (social, video, podcast) where the daily life of the community happens. Use it as a directory: bookmark what you need, ignore what you don't, and return when you want to go deeper.

---

## 1. Official Channels

The single most authoritative source for everything Zentangle is the official organization itself.

### zentangle.com

The organization's main site is the canonical place for: the Project Pack series, store products (tiles, pens, kits, books), CZT directory and certification information, the Zentangle Academy online platform, the official blog (zentangle.com/blogs/blog), and announcements about events such as ZenAgain.

The **official blog** is updated regularly with posts from Rick, Maria, and family members covering history, philosophy, new tangles, project tutorials, and community spotlights. It is one of the few places where the founders' voices appear directly.

### Zentangle YouTube

The official Zentangle YouTube channel hosts video tutorials, tangle demonstrations, project pack previews, and recorded talks. Rick and Maria's on-camera presence is gentle and unhurried — characteristic of the method itself.

### Project Packs (zentangle.com/collections/project-packs)

The Project Pack series is the official replacement for "events": themed kits with daily videos that let participants tangle along in real time. **Project Pack No. 29** (April 2026) is the current release; the series will continue with new packs at intervals. Older packs remain available indefinitely.

### Zentangle Mosaic App

The **Zentangle Mosaic app** is the official mobile application for sharing tiles within the community. As of 2026, the app remains active on both Apple App Store and Google Play, with both free and subscription tiers. Subscribers gain access to features such as Kitchen Table Tangles videos and the Tangle Step Out Library. Reports of the app's "discontinuation" that occasionally circulate online are inaccurate; the app continues to be developed and supported.

### Zentangle Academy

The Zentangle Academy online platform was developed in part to support hybrid attendance at ZenAgain 2025 and now hosts streaming classes and recorded sessions for the wider CZT community.

## 2. TanglePatterns.com — The Community's Pattern Dictionary

If zentangle.com is the organization's home, **TanglePatterns.com** is the community's central reference work.

Run by **Linda Farmer (CZT)**, TanglePatterns.com is, simply, the global tangle dictionary. Linda has been indexing tangle patterns since 2010, and her site catalogs thousands of patterns submitted by tanglers and CZTs around the world, each with a step-by-step ("step-out") demonstration.

### How TanglePatterns.com Is Organized

- **Alphabetical pattern index** — every tangle, organized by name, with step-outs and source attribution
- **Linda's List of Official Tangle Patterns** — a curated subset of patterns published officially by Zentangle HQ
- **Tag clouds and categories** — search by visual feel: grid-based, organic, linear, etc.
- **Weekly TanglePatterns Strings (TPS)** — Linda's signature weekly prompt
- **Author pages** — every contributor has a page collecting their submitted tangles
- **The annual Tangle Guide** — a downloadable PDF compendium of every tangle indexed up to that year, refreshed each January. The **2026 Edition** is the current release.

### Subscription Model

The site itself is free to read. Linda offers paid resources, primarily:
- **The TanglePatterns.com Tangle Guide** — annual PDF, paid download, refreshed each January
- **Stepouts E-Book** — collected step-by-step pattern guides

Linda funds the site partly through these resources and partly through donations. She also publishes a free weekly newsletter (the "Tickled to Tangle" mailing) summarizing new patterns added.

### Why TanglePatterns Matters

For more than fifteen years, every newly invented tangle has had one place where it could be canonized: Linda's site. The cultural function is enormous. When tanglers cite a tangle by name, they are usually citing it through TanglePatterns.com. The site is, effectively, the community's collective memory.

## 3. Major Individual Blogs and Teacher Sites

A handful of individual CZTs and tangle teachers have built bodies of work that function as pillars of the community.

### Beckah Krahula

Author of **One Zentangle a Day**, the most widely sold introductory book on the method. Beckah's structured 6-week curriculum is the standard recommendation for beginners who learn well from books.

### Sandy Steen Bartholomew (Bumble Beans)

A pioneer in the early days, Sandy authored several influential books including **Totally Tangled** and **Yoga for Your Brain**. Her Bumble Beans blog remains an archive of early-era tangles and approaches.

### Margaret Bremner — Enthusiastic Artist

Margaret's blog **Enthusiastic Artist** focuses on Zendalas (round zendala-format tiles) and on the meditative aspects of practice. Her posts are deeply reflective and serve as a model of how a long-term tangle journal looks.

### Helen Williams (Australia)

Helen's tangle blog has long served as a touchstone for elegant pattern design and for clear, photographable step-outs. Her Australian community presence has helped seed the practice in the southern hemisphere.

### Cris Letourneau

CZT and prolific writer whose detailed pattern explorations and tutorial sequences are widely referenced.

### Tomas Padros

Spanish CZT whose contributions span pattern invention, ZIA work, and bilingual community-building.

### Eni Oken

For tanglers who want to push into **advanced ZIA**, Eni Oken's site (enioken.com) is the destination. Eni teaches sophisticated 3D shading, fractal compositions, embellished dragons, tangled landscapes, and other techniques that go well beyond classic Zentangle. Her courses are sold through enioken.teachable.com and represent some of the most rigorous instruction available.

## 4. Social Media: Where Daily Life Happens

### Instagram

Instagram is the dominant gallery space for daily tangle sharing. The most important hashtags:
- **#zentangle** — the broadest tag
- **#zentangleart** — closely related
- **#zia** — Zentangle-Inspired Art (color, composition, mixed media)
- **#zentangleinspiredart** — the long-form version of #zia
- **#tangleart** — broader still, often includes adjacent doodling
- **#czt** — work by Certified Zentangle Teachers

Following these hashtags gives an excellent sense of how the practice looks across the world in real time.

### Pinterest

Pinterest functions as a massive ZIA gallery and reference library. Boards collecting tangle step-outs, ZIA inspiration, and Zendala layouts are extremely useful for visual research.

### Facebook Groups

- **Square One: Purely Zentangle** — the largest classic-format group; weekly tangle focus
- **Square One Zentangle BaZaar** — sister group for ZIA and related work
- **Official Zentangle community** — followers of the zentangle.com pages
- **Country and language-specific groups** — Japanese, Korean, Chinese, German, French, Spanish, etc.

### Reddit

The **r/Zentangle** subreddit is a smaller but engaged community where tanglers share work, ask technique questions, and discuss tools. The vibe is friendly and beginner-tolerant. It is a good gentle on-ramp for those who prefer Reddit's threaded format to Facebook's feed.

### Discord

Various tangle-focused Discord servers exist, often run by individual CZTs. Discord communities tend to be small and intimate, useful for accountability practice and live tangle-along sessions.

## 6. Podcasts

Podcasting around Zentangle is modest but real. Rick and Maria have appeared on:
- **NAEA** (National Art Education Association) podcast episodes
- **Susquehanna Alchemy** with Michael Wann
- **Better PR** with Kathie Taylor and Deb Page (CZTs)

A handful of CZT-led podcasts and audio interviews appear sporadically across YouTube and podcast platforms. There is currently no high-frequency dedicated Zentangle podcast — the community's primary audio life happens inside YouTube tutorial voiceovers.

## For Beginners: A 4-Step Entry Sequence

If you are brand new to Zentangle and want a structured way in, here is a sequence that reliably works.

### Step 1 — Start at zentangle.com

Read the "What is Zentangle?" section. Watch one or two of Rick and Maria's introductory videos. Get the official authentic kit if you can — official tiles, a Sakura Pigma Micron pen, and a graphite pencil. Beginning with proper materials matters more than people expect.

### Step 2 — Read **One Zentangle a Day** by Beckah Krahula

This is the most-recommended beginner book in the community. It walks you through six weeks of small daily exercises, introducing tangles gradually and building confidence. By the end you will know perhaps 30 tangles well enough to draw them from memory.

### Step 3 — Make TanglePatterns.com your daily home

Bookmark Linda Farmer's site. Browse the alphabetical pattern index. Try a new tangle each day. Subscribe to the weekly TanglePatterns Strings (TPS) prompt. Consider buying the current annual Tangle Guide PDF — at the price of a small book, it gives you a permanent reference of every indexed tangle.

### Step 4 — Join a community challenge

Choose **one** of: Square One: Purely Zentangle (Facebook), Diva Challenge (blog), or the r/Zentangle subreddit. Begin posting your tiles. Don't post for praise — post to participate. The community will gently teach you the rest.

That's it. After three to six months working this sequence, you will know how to tangle, where to find new patterns, who to follow, and which corner of the community fits you best. Everything beyond that — ZIA, advanced ZIA via Eni Oken, becoming a CZT, exhibitions, regional events — opens up naturally from there.

The Zentangle world is large but never gatekept. Walk in, pick up a pen, and you are already in.
