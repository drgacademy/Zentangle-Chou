# 10 — Taiwan's Zentangle Ecosystem

> A primer for someone outside Taiwan curious about how Zentangle took root and grew on the island — who teaches it, who reads it, where it shows up in public life, and where the next opportunities sit. As of May 2026.

---

## How Zentangle arrived in Taiwan

Zentangle reached Taiwan via three channels almost simultaneously, between roughly 2010 and 2015. The first channel was direct certification: a small group of Taiwanese artists travelled to Massachusetts to attend Rick Roberts and Maria Thomas's CZT (Certified Zentangle Teacher) seminars and brought the practice home with full credentials. The second was translation: Taiwanese publishers acquired Chinese-language rights to the foundational books and to several early popularisations from the US and Europe. The third was social media: Pinterest and Facebook surfaced the practice to a Taiwanese audience already primed by a strong stationery culture and a deep interest in Japanese-style mindful crafts (whitework embroidery, washi tape journaling, and the broader 文具控 / stationery-fan subculture).

The result is unusual. Taiwan has a denser per-capita population of CZTs than almost any non-Western country, an active local books market, and a teaching infrastructure that runs through community colleges (社區大學) as well as private studios. Within Asia, only Hong Kong rivals Taiwan as a Zentangle hub, and Hong Kong's scene is heavily influenced by Taiwanese teachers and translators.

## The first generation: 蘿拉老師 (Laura Liu) and 戴安老師 (Diane Tai)

The acknowledged anchor of Taiwan's Zentangle scene is Laura Liu (蘿拉老師), generally credited as the first Taiwan-based Certified Zentangle Teacher. Laura had twenty years of stamping-art teaching experience in Taiwan, the United States, and Shanghai before being certified as CZT (cohorts 4 and 17). She has been actively teaching Zentangle in Taiwan since 2010, developing localised lesson plans that adapt the original Massachusetts curriculum to Taiwanese learners.

Laura is also the author and translator behind much of the Taiwanese book market for Zentangle:

- 《蘿拉老師教你畫禪繞（入門篇）》— Laura's own beginner book; a revised second edition is in print
- 《禪繞畫之書》— Chinese translation of *The Book of Zentangle* (Roberts & Thomas), translated jointly by Laura, 戴寧 and 戴安

Her daughter, **Diane Tai (戴安)**, is herself a CZT (cohort 11) and currently a part-time lecturer in the English Department at Shih Hsin University. The mother–daughter team has run more than a hundred classes between them and trained thousands of students. They operate teaching pages including 想享學, and Laura's books are stocked widely on books.com.tw, Kingstone, and Readmoo.

This first-generation pairing is important because it set the Taiwanese tone: bilingual (Chinese-English), book-anchored, methodologically faithful to the original Zentangle curriculum, and willing to teach across age groups from corporate workshops to senior-centre classes.

## Sarah Wu (莎拉) — the Henna + Zentangle bridge

The other widely-recognised Taiwanese figure is **Sarah Wu (莎拉)**, founder of **繪花藝術學院 (Henna Art Academy)** at sarah-henna.com. Sarah's entry point was unusual: she encountered both Henna body painting and Zentangle in 2013, recognised the line-vocabulary overlap, and built a studio practice that crosses both disciplines.

## Other Taiwan CZTs and teaching organisations

Beyond Laura, Diane, and Sarah, Taiwan has several active CZTs and a formal organisational layer:

- **財團法人禪繞文教基金會 (CZA Foundation / Chinese Zentangle Academy Foundation)** — registered in Taoyuan, the foundation's stated mission is to carry forward the vision of co-founders Rick Roberts and Maria Thomas in a Chinese-speaking context. It runs the cza.org.tw site and partners with the regional CZT-Asia seminar program.
- **CZT-Asia Seminars (czt-asia.cwlinc.com.tw)** — operated by 好心藝 (Caring Art / CWL Inc.), this is the official Asia-region Zentangle seminar host. CZT certification courses for Mandarin speakers run in Taiwan, taught with full Mandarin instruction (with online attendance available). Hosting CZT-Asia in Taiwan is the structural reason the island has so many credentialed teachers.
- **台灣禪繞教師協會 (Taiwan CZT Association)** — a Facebook-anchored community of CZT teachers and enthusiasts.

Other names that appear repeatedly in Taiwanese Zentangle community discussions include Marion (a CZT with a YouTube portfolio in Mandarin), Jo (the JO愛畫禪繞 community-college course on the New Taipei City community college network), and a long tail of CZTs running small studios or community-college classes. Searching the Zentangle company's official find-a-CZT directory at zentangle.com/pages/find-a-czt, filtered for Taiwan, returns dozens of certified teachers as of 2026.

## Workshops, studios, and community colleges

Zentangle teaching in Taiwan runs in three tiers:

**Private studios** charge premium prices for small-group teaching. Sarah's 繪花藝術學院, Laura's classes via 想享學 and OMIA Plus, and a number of independent CZT studios fall here.

**Community colleges (社區大學)** are the most affordable route for Taiwanese learners. The Taipei Community College Network (ccwt.tp.edu.tw), the New Taipei City Community College Network (cci.ntpc.edu.tw), and the National Community College Information Network (cc.moe.edu.tw) all list Zentangle courses semester by semester, typically 12–18 weeks in length. The New Taipei system in particular has hosted long-running courses such as JO愛畫禪繞.

**Cultural extension centres** at universities — most consistently Chinese Culture University's推廣教育中心 (Taipei and Taichung campuses) — have offered Zentangle and Henna programs since at least 2015.

**Coffee shops and cultural venues** host single-evening or weekend workshops as social activities. The 華山1914 / 華山文創園區 cluster in Taipei, the Songshan Cultural and Creative Park, and Eslite (誠品) bookstore-affiliated event spaces have all hosted Zentangle workshops or pop-up sessions.

## Pricing landscape (verified ranges, May 2026)

Pricing varies sharply by tier:

- **Single-day taster workshop (體驗課程)**: NT$1,200 – NT$3,500 depending on venue, materials, and teacher seniority. Around NT$2,000 is a typical mid-market rate.
- **Multi-week private studio course (4–8 sessions)**: NT$5,000 – NT$15,000. CZT certified teachers in private studios sit at the upper end.
- **Community college semester course (12–18 weeks)**: typically NT$2,500 – NT$4,500 plus a small materials fee. This is by far the cheapest way to take a sustained course.
- **CZT-Asia certification seminar**: comparable to the US seminar — running into the high four-digit US-dollar range (low NT$100,000s) when priced for Taiwan, plus materials.

Numbers above are approximations based on current public listings; individual studios and colleges price differently.

## Taiwanese Zentangle books — Traditional Chinese editions

The Taiwanese book market for Zentangle is dense for a niche craft. A non-exhaustive list of titles found on books.com.tw (博客來):

- 《蘿拉老師教你畫禪繞（入門篇）》— Laura Liu — 24 base-line practices and pattern progression; a revised second edition is in print.

- 《心靜禪繞畫：一筆一畫，靜心紓壓》— focused on the calming/stress-reduction framing.

- 《放手畫禪繞：我的108種禪繞畫練習》— 108-pattern practice book.
- 《抒心禪繞畫：104款必學圖樣＋完整繪製教學》— 104-pattern teaching book.
- 《100個禪繞畫的創意練習：從入門到進階、七大類圖樣、最新黑白與彩色禪繞畫法》— pattern catalogue with colour techniques.

Several of these are translations of European or US originals; Laura's books are the best-known native Taiwanese authorship.

## Public events and visibility

Zentangle in Taiwan shows up in public places:

- **華山1914 文創園區 (Huashan 1914 Creative Park, Taipei)** and **松山文創園區 (Songshan Cultural and Creative Park)** host pop-up workshops and market booths during weekend and seasonal markets.
- **誠品 (Eslite)** bookstore branches stock the books listed above and have run Zentangle author events — Eslite's events arm has historically programmed mindful-craft sessions.
- **Coffee-shop workshops** are common in Taipei, Taichung, Tainan, and Kaohsiung — typically a one-evening NT$800–1,500 introductory session.
- **Corporate wellness commissions** — Sarah's studio explicitly markets to companies for stress-relief workshops, and similar commissions are now commonplace for CZTs across the island.
- **Taoyuan International Youth Creative Aesthetics Competition** (Sarah Wu has served as chief Henna-category judge) — adjacent to but not solely Zentangle, this competition signals public-sector recognition of the broader contemplative-drawing space.

## Demographics — who actually takes Zentangle in Taiwan

Field observation of class rosters and CZT social media gives a consistent picture:

- **Gender**: predominantly women, roughly 80–90% across most settings.
- **Age**: 25–60 is the core, with strong upper-end participation in 60+ community-college classes and senior-centre programs.
- **Occupation**: skewed toward design professionals (illustration, graphic design, packaging, interior), educators (kindergarten through high school art teachers), healthcare workers (nurses and OTs particularly), counsellors and therapists, and homemakers.
- **Reason for attending**: stress reduction is the most-cited entry motivation; "I always wanted to draw but never had talent" is the second most common.

This demographic profile matters for anyone planning new Zentangle programming on the island: messaging that lands with a 35–55-year-old female educator or healthcare worker is the most reliable target.

## Crossovers — where Taiwanese Zentangle is hybridising

Three hybrid spaces stand out:

**Zentangle + Henna**. Sarah Wu's studio is the headline example, but the cross-pollination runs both ways — Zentangle students learning Henna for body-painting events, and Henna artists discovering Zentangle as a paper practice.

**Zentangle + 心理諮商 (psychological counselling)**. A growing number of Taiwanese counsellors and clinical psychologists are training in Zentangle as a between-session tool, particularly for anxiety and stress clients. The CZT-Asia seminar attracts a notable cohort of mental-health practitioners.

**Zentangle + 親子手作 (parent-child crafting)**. Family-focused workshops at parks, libraries, and school holiday programs use Zentangle as an accessible joint activity. The structural reason: it works equally well for an adult and a child seated at the same table.

A fourth, smaller hybrid is **Zentangle + 曼陀羅 (mandala)**, formalised in Sarah Wu's registered 立體水玉曼陀羅® and 線構曼陀羅® formats, and informally elsewhere.

## Future opportunities

Looking ahead from May 2026:

- **Digital classes**: Taiwan already has OMIA Plus and 想享學 anchoring paid online video. The next wave will be subscription-style ongoing programming with monthly tangle drops and community feedback loops.
- **Mental-wellness app integration**: no Taiwanese mental-wellness app has yet bundled Zentangle as a structured module, despite the obvious fit.
- **School programs**: Zentangle for SEL is a credible expansion path, especially in private elementary schools where the curriculum has flexibility.
- **Senior care contracts**: Taiwan's long-term care reform creates structural demand for low-cost, motor-friendly group activities. Zentangle fits the brief.
- **Tourism-craft integration**: cultural-tourism tile-painting workshops aimed at international visitors (especially Japanese and Hong Kong tourists) are an under-served market.

**Where to read (books)**:
- 博客來 (books.com.tw) — search 禪繞畫
- 金石堂 (kingstone.com.tw) — same search
- Readmoo and Kobo Taiwan — for Laura's e-book editions
- 誠品 (Eslite) — physical browsing in Taipei, Taichung, Kaohsiung branches

**Where to buy supplies**:
- **Sakura Pigma Micron pens (01 / 005 / 02)**: available at 誠品 stationery sections, 誠格美術 (artfans.com.tw), 月光創意文具 (Moonlight Creative — the official Taiwan distributor of Sakura), Shopee Taiwan, and books.com.tw stationery.
- **Tiles**: Authentic Zentangle tiles are imported through CZTs; for practice, white 300 gsm watercolour paper cut to 3.5×3.5 inches is a common substitute, available at any 文具行 (stationery shop) or art supply stores like artshop.com.tw.
- **Pencils and tortillons**: any standard art-supply outlet — 誠品文具, 光南文具批發, or chain stationery shops in Taipei's 後火車站 area.

**Where to find a CZT**:
- zentangle.com/pages/find-a-czt — official global directory, filterable to Taiwan
- 台灣禪繞教師協會 Facebook page — peer-recommended teachers

The island's Zentangle scene is small enough that within two or three searches you can usually reach a CZT directly. For most Taiwanese learners curious about getting started, the practical recommendation is: read one of Laura Liu's books from books.com.tw, buy a Micron 01 from 誠品 or Moonlight, sign up for either a one-day Sarah Wu introductory workshop or a community-college semester class, and join one of the Facebook groups. That sequence is fast, affordable, and culturally calibrated for Taiwanese learners.
