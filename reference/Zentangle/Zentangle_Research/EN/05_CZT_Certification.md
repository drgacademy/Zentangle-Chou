# 05 — CZT Certification: The Path to Becoming a Certified Zentangle Teacher

## What "CZT" Stands For

**CZT** is short for **Certified Zentangle Teacher**. It is the only credential officially issued by Zentangle, Inc. — the company founded by Rick Roberts and Maria Thomas — that grants a person the right to use the Zentangle® trademark, to teach the Zentangle Method in classes and workshops, and to be listed in the official global directory at [zentangle.com/pages/find-a-czt](https://zentangle.com/pages/find-a-czt).

There is no online quiz, take-home exam, or self-paced certificate that can confer CZT status. The credential is awarded only after a multi-day, instructor-led seminar conducted (or licensed) by Rick and Maria themselves. This deliberate "in-person, by the founders" model is one of the program's defining features and the main reason the Zentangle community has stayed unusually coherent for an art form that has spread to dozens of countries.

## A Short History of the Program

The Zentangle Method was created in 2003–2004 by Rick Roberts (a former monk) and Maria Thomas (a professional letterer). After several years of teaching small groups out of their home studio in Whitinsville, Massachusetts, demand grew faster than the founders could meet on their own. In **2006**, they ran the first formal Certified Zentangle Teacher seminar — what is now retroactively called **CZT #1** — to train a cohort of ambassadors who could carry the Method to their own communities.

From that first seminar onward, every CZT class has been numbered sequentially. The numbering is more than bookkeeping: CZTs identify themselves on bios, websites, and business cards as "CZT #19" or "CZT #34," and the number telegraphs roughly when they joined the lineage. By 2026 the program has reached the **mid-40s**: CZT #43 took place in 2025, and **CZT #44 is scheduled for 2026** as both an in-person seminar in New England and an online cohort.

Across these forty-plus seminars, Rick and Maria — joined increasingly by their daughter Martha and son-in-law Eli, plus a small circle of senior CZT mentors — have personally trained an estimated **4,000+ CZTs** in over forty countries. The exact running total is not published; informal counts inside the community put it between 4,000 and 5,000 active certificate holders.

## Where CZT Seminars Are Held

### New England (the original home)

For many years, every seminar took place near the founders' home in Whitinsville, Massachusetts, with classes held at a hotel conference center in nearby Providence, Rhode Island, and later in Sturbridge and Mystic, Connecticut. Attending a seminar in New England is still considered the "classic" experience — students often visit Rick and Maria's studio, share meals with the Roberts–Thomas family, and meet the artists whose tangles they have been drawing for years.

### CZT-Asia (Mandarin-language seminars)

Beginning in the mid-2010s, the founders licensed **Created With Love (CWL)** — a Taiwan-based company also operating as Chinese Zentangle Academy (CZA) — to deliver an officially sanctioned seminar in Mandarin Chinese. These are branded **CZT-Asia Seminars** and follow the same curriculum, ceremony, and certification standards as the New England seminars, with Rick and Maria participating live (in person and via video) and CWL's senior CZTs translating and co-facilitating.

Recent CZT-Asia seminars have been held in **Shanghai, China** — for example **CZT-Asia #9 (August)** and **CZT-Asia #10 (December)** in recent calendars — and the program rotates through other Asian cities when logistics allow. Participants from **Taiwan, mainland China, Hong Kong, Macau, Singapore, Malaysia, Japan, and South Korea** regularly attend.

### Germany and the Netherlands

Although Zentangle does not run a permanent European seminar series, single seminars have been hosted in **Germany** through licensed partners, and a strong CZT community in **the Netherlands** flies CZTs to either New England or Asia.

### Online Seminars

Since the COVID-19 era, every numbered seminar has also offered a parallel **online cohort**. CZT #44, for example, runs simultaneously as an in-person seminar and as a fully online seminar with live Zoom sessions, pre-recorded video modules, and a mailed supply kit. Online CZTs receive the same certificate and the same listing in the directory.

## Format: A 3.5-Day Total Immersion

A CZT seminar is intentionally compressed. The standard format runs **three and a half days**, starting late afternoon on Day 1 and ending around lunchtime on Day 4. Days are long — typically 8 a.m. to 9 or 10 p.m., with meals taken together — and the schedule mixes drawing practice, lecture, ceremony, group discussion, and small-group teaching exercises.

A typical day:

- **Morning** — meditation or grounding exercise; a tangle demonstration by Rick or Maria; supervised practice.
- **Mid-morning** — lecture on history, philosophy, or the eight-step method.
- **Afternoon** — teaching methodology lab: students take turns delivering a mini-lesson and receive feedback.
- **Evening** — community session, "tangle exchange," storytelling, or a guided drawing under candlelight.

Class size is capped (historically around 100 in person, sometimes more online), and seats often sell out within hours of registration opening.

## Curriculum Overview

The seminar covers six broad areas:

1. **History and origins** — how Rick and Maria discovered the method, the philosophical lineage from calligraphy and Zen practice, and why the trademark and ceremony matter.
2. **The Eight Steps** — gratitude and appreciation, corner dots, border, string, tangle, shade, initial and sign, appreciate. Every CZT must be able to teach these in order, in their own words.
3. **Core tangles** — the founder-released "official" tangles such as Crescent Moon, Hollibaugh, Printemps, Tipple, Florz, Static, and others, plus instruction on how to learn and teach new tangles.
4. **Teaching methodology** — how to set up a class, what supplies to recommend, how to pace a 90-minute introductory workshop, how to handle students who say "I can't draw," and how to license-respectfully introduce yourself as a CZT.
5. **Zentangle culture and ceremony** — the deliberate slowness, the use of ceremony (lighting a candle, reading a passage, ringing a bell), and the language of "deliberate stroke" and "no mistakes, only opportunities."
6. **Business and ethics of teaching** — proper trademark use, what a CZT may and may not call "Zentangle," pricing, supply sourcing, and ongoing engagement with the community.

By the end of Day 4 every student has taught at least one mini-lesson, drawn dozens of tiles, and participated in a closing ceremony in which Rick and Maria personally hand each new CZT their certificate.

## Cost

Pricing has risen modestly over the years. For **CZT #44 (2026)** the published rates are:

- **New students:** **US $1,995** total (a $485 deposit plus $1,500 balance, taxes additional).
- **Returning CZTs** (anyone who has previously completed a CZT seminar in the US, Germany, Asia, or online): **US $1,500**.

These figures cover all classroom sessions, supplemental video content, and the supplies kit. Travel, lodging, and meals are **not** included. Most US-based participants budget an **additional US $1,000–1,500** for a New England hotel and flights, and international participants commonly budget **US $2,500–4,500** total when factoring in airfare. CZT-Asia seminars in Shanghai are priced in the equivalent of roughly RMB 12,000–15,000.

A **scholarship application** opens around January 1 of each seminar year for a small number of needs-based and diversity-based seats, administered through the Zentangle Foundation.

> Pricing changes annually. Always confirm current rates on the official seminar registration page at [zentangle.com](https://zentangle.com/) before budgeting.

## Prerequisites

The official prerequisites are deliberately light:

- **A genuine love of Zentangle** — applicants are expected to have practiced the method at home and engaged with the community.
- **An intention to teach** — the program is not designed as personal enrichment for hobbyists. Applicants are asked to describe how they plan to teach (workshops, schools, hospitals, libraries, online, etc.).
- **Willingness to commit** to the full 3.5 days, the ceremony, and the lifelong code of conduct.

There is no required portfolio review and no minimum age for adults. Several CZTs have certified in their seventies and eighties; many have certified as second careers after retirement from teaching, healthcare, or design.

## After Certification: What a CZT Receives

Once Rick and Maria sign the certificate, a new CZT gains:

- **A printed certificate** with their unique CZT number (e.g., "CZT #44-127").
- **Trademark license** to use "Certified Zentangle Teacher®" and the Zentangle® logo on teaching materials, websites, and business cards, subject to brand guidelines.
- **Listing in the official global CZT directory** at [zentangle.com/pages/find-a-czt](https://zentangle.com/pages/find-a-czt), searchable by country, region, and name.
- **Access to the CZT-only resource library**, which includes printable handouts, branded teaching aids, advance notice of new tangles, and a private newsletter from Rick and Maria.
- **Discount on supplies and on future CZT seminars** (the $1,500 returning-student rate).
- **Eligibility to attend ZenAgain.**

## ZenAgain — the Annual CZT Reunion

**ZenAgain** is the annual in-person reunion event for current CZTs only. It is part conference, part retreat, part family reunion. ZenAgain 2025 was held at the Mystic Marriott in Mystic, Connecticut, drawing **396 CZTs** in person and online. The next confirmed event is **ZenAgain 2027, October 24–27**, also at Mystic Marriott, with a livestream option for those who cannot travel.

Programming includes new-tangle reveals, advanced technique workshops, panels on teaching, an art exhibition, and a closing ceremony. Many CZTs treat ZenAgain as their primary continuing-education investment of the year.

## Geographic Distribution of CZTs

The CZT directory is published with no central country count, but informal community estimates and the country-by-country lists maintained at [TanglePatterns.com](https://tanglepatterns.com/zentangles/teaching-zentangle%C2%AE/list-of-czts-by-country) suggest:

- **United States** — the largest cohort by far, with strong concentrations in New England, the Pacific Northwest, California, and Texas.
- **Europe** — particularly **Germany** and **the Netherlands**, with smaller communities in the UK, France, Italy, Belgium, Switzerland, Spain, and Scandinavia.
- **Asia** — **Taiwan**, **mainland China**, **Hong Kong**, **Singapore**, **Malaysia**, **Japan**, and **South Korea** all have active CZT populations. Japan and Taiwan are the two most established Asian communities.
- **Oceania** — Australia and New Zealand each host a few dozen CZTs, with Tasmania-based **Helen Williams** widely credited with building the regional community.
- **Latin America and Africa** — small but growing, with notable CZTs in Brazil, Mexico, and South Africa.

## Asia in Focus

### Taiwan

Taiwan is widely regarded as one of the most active CZT countries in Asia. Two figures stand out:

- **Laura Liu (蘿拉)** — Taiwan's **first CZT**, certified around 2010. She has decades of color-printing and handcraft experience across Taiwan, the United States, and Shanghai, and has authored the bestselling "蘿拉老師教你畫禪繞" (entry-level) series. Laura is the matriarch of the Taiwanese Zentangle community.
- **Diana Tai (戴安)** — Laura's daughter, **CZT #11** (one of the earliest non-North-American CZTs), holder of a master's degree in English Language Teaching from Hawaii Pacific University, and a former adjunct lecturer at Shih Hsin University. Together with her mother she has taught more than a hundred classes and thousands of students. Her "戴安老師教你畫禪繞" advanced-level books cover step-by-step shading, the kaleidoscope (Zendala) method, and color application.

Other notable Taiwan-based CZTs include teachers affiliated with the **台灣禪繞教師協會 (Taiwan Zentangle Teachers Association)** and the **財團法人禪繞文教基金會 (Zentangle Art and Education Foundation)**, which acts as the de-facto Taiwanese coordinating body.

The dedicated Taiwan-language seminar registration portal is at [czt-asia.cwlinc.com.tw](https://czt-asia.cwlinc.com.tw/), operated by Created With Love. Sarah Henna ([sarah-henna.com](https://www.sarah-henna.com)) is also frequently cited inside the community as an active Taiwanese CZT, though directory listings should be reconfirmed on zentangle.com.

### Japan

Japan has one of the strongest CZT communities outside the United States, with active teachers including **Akemi Nibe** and **Kuniko Tanase**, both well known for advanced shading techniques and for translating tangles into a distinctly Japanese aesthetic. Japan-based CZTs typically attend either CZT-Asia (Shanghai) or fly to the New England seminar.

### South Korea

South Korea's community is smaller but rapidly growing, with most Korean CZTs trained through CZT-Asia.

### Mainland China and Hong Kong

Mainland China's CZT pipeline is overwhelmingly funneled through **Created With Love / Chinese Zentangle Academy**, which runs the Mandarin seminar. Hong Kong has its own boutique studios (e.g., Mi.TangleArt, Desk-One) where multiple CZTs teach.

## How to Apply

1. Visit **[zentangle.com](https://zentangle.com/)** and navigate to "Become a CZT."
2. Watch for the seminar registration page (e.g., "CZT 44 Registration"). Registration typically opens **6–9 months in advance** and fills quickly.
3. For Mandarin-language seminars, register via **[czt-asia.cwlinc.com.tw](https://czt-asia.cwlinc.com.tw/)**.
4. Submit the registration form, which asks about your background, your reason for wanting to teach, and your community.
5. Pay the deposit to secure a seat. The balance is due closer to the seminar date.
6. Receive a pre-seminar packet with reading materials and supply guidance.
7. Attend.

## Why the Model Endures

In an age of self-paced online certificates, the CZT program's insistence on in-person ceremony, founder-led teaching, and strict trademark protection looks almost old-fashioned. That, however, is the point. By making the credential personal, slow, and emotionally weighty, Rick and Maria have built a global community that protects the integrity of the Method itself. A CZT is not just a person who has learned to draw tangles — they are someone who has been welcomed, in person, into a lineage. That is the credential's quiet superpower, and the reason 4,000-plus people in forty-plus countries continue to identify by their CZT number for the rest of their lives.
